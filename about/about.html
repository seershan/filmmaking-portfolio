Introduction

I am a film student and visual storyteller focused on human emotions.

I am a student filmmaker, photographer, and visual storyteller based in Pune, India. My work explores atmosphere, memory, people, and places through short films, documentaries, portrait photography, and visual narratives. What began as a curiosity about cinema gradually became a way of understanding and expressing ideas through images, sound, and storytelling. I enjoy creating work that feels personal, cinematic, and emotionally grounded. Whether through a photograph, a documentary, or a short film, I am interested in capturing moments that reveal something about the people, places, and experiences around us. This portfolio brings together those projects and reflects my ongoing journey of learning, experimenting, and refining my voice as a filmmaker.

My Journey

A timeline of learning, making, and refining the visual language.

My journey into filmmaking didn't begin with a dream of becoming a filmmaker. It began with a desire to become someone different every time I watched a film. After watching 3 Idiots, I wanted to be a filmmaker. After Kabir Singh, I wanted to be a doctor. After Lakshya, I wanted to join the army. Every film introduced me to a new life, a new perspective, and a new profession. Over time, I realized I couldn't be everything. What inspired me wasn't the profession itself, but the people bringing those characters to life. I thought acting might be the answer because, as an actor, I could experience many different lives. But eventually I understood that actors tell stories written by others, while filmmakers have the opportunity to tell stories of their own. That realization led me to filmmaking. My first step came after watching an interview with filmmaker Srijit Mukherji. He said that everyone who loves cinema should make at least one film, whether it turns out good or bad. That idea stayed with me, and on that day I decided to make my first short film. Since then, I have made eight short films. Looking back, the best decision I made was simply starting. Not waiting for the perfect camera, the perfect team, or the perfect opportunity. One of the biggest lessons I learned was that I often built stories around the actors, locations, and resources I already had. At the time, that was necessary because I was working with almost no budget and limited options. Those constraints taught me how to make films with whatever was available. Today, my approach is different. The story comes first. Once the story is clear, I look for the right cast, locations, and creative choices to serve it. I'm still learning, still experimenting, and still making mistakes. But every project brings me closer to understanding the kind of filmmaker I want to become.
